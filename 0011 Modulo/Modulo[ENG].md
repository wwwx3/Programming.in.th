## Modulo

**Time limit:** 1 second  
**Memory limit:** 32 megabytes  

For two integers A and B, A modulo B is the remainder when A is divided by B.  
For example, 7, 14, 27, 38 modulo 3 give 1, 2, 0, and 2, respectively.

You are given 10 integers. Compute each number modulo 42 and determine how many distinct remainders there are.

---

### Input  
The input consists of 10 lines, each containing one positive integer less than 1,000.

---

### Output  
Print a single integer: the number of distinct remainders when the 10 numbers are taken modulo 42.

---

### Examples  

| Input | Output |
|------|--------|
| 1<br>2<br>3<br>4<br>5<br>6<br>7<br>8<br>9<br>10 | 10 |

| Input | Output |
|------|--------|
| 42<br>84<br>252<br>420<br>840<br>126<br>42<br>84<br>420<br>126 | 1 |

| Input | Output |
|------|--------|
| 39<br>40<br>41<br>42<br>43<br>44<br>82<br>83<br>84<br>85 | 6 |
